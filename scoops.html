Scoops News App

November 2024

Tools: Figma

The problem + Our solution

“How might we develop a news app that is minimalistic and visually appealing that allows personalization for the user that still helps provide daily news?”

Our solution is a minimalistic news app that allows users to personalize what content is suggested for them, and provides unique features to improve their news consumption experience.

Research

Survey - 20 responses

  • Average age: 20 years
  • Most used news apps/websites: NYT, Apple News, Twitter, Instagram
  • Average use time: 4-5 times per week

Why do you use a news app?

“I want to stay up to date with current events and whatever is going on in the world.”

What topics/types of news do you generally look/read about?

“Entertainment/Pop Culture, Foreign affairs, Politics, Science, Weather, Local News, Cooking …”

Do you prefer general news or specialized themes?

“It depends; I think having a mix is good.”

“There are certain topics that I prefer, like politics.”

What is a feature of the app that you couldn’t/could live without?

“I really like the Trending/Today News page that shows highlights and relevant news.”

“I could live without subscriptions and constant ads.”

Competitive Analysis

New York times

  • Too Many Features - Takes too many clicks; takes long to scroll to a category
  • Cognitive overload; too much information - Especially for users without the upgrade

Apple News

  • Pop-up ads appear too often
  • Cognitive overload especially on the “Today” page
  • Too many options/features to choose from

Shades News

  • Audio/podcasts are only limited for the day of - Users may want to explore older podcasts
  • Information is too summarized - Especially for users who want to still read an article
